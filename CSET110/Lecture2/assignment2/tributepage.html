Sade


Sade is a British band known for its smooth, soulful sound that blends elements of R&B, jazz, and pop. Formed in 1982, the group is fronted by Nigerian-British singer Sade Adu. Their music is characterized by its lush arrangements and Adu's distinctive, sultry voice.

Their debut album, Diamond Life (1984), was a huge success, featuring hits like "Smooth Operator" and "Hang on to Your Love." Over the years, they've released several acclaimed albums, including Promise (1985), Stronger Than Pride (1988), and Love Deluxe (1992).

Sade is known for a distinctive style that remains influential, often associated with a cool, sophisticated vibe. Despite their success, they are known for their relatively low profile and selective touring. Sade Adu, in particular, has been noted for her elusive nature, contributing to the band's enigmatic appeal.
